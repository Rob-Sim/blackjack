Blackjack (or 21) is a card game in which the objective is to attempt to reach as close to the number 21 as possible. You will recieve your initial cards and use your intuition to decipher whether or not you should take another card and raise your score in order to get closer to 21 - or whether you should stick at your current number. You are trying to get closer to the objective than your opponent to win the game!
Cards are worth their displayed value, other than picture cards in which are worth 10 each. Such cards are the King, Queen, and Jack. The Ace card's value is determined based upon your current score. If you are able take 11 to your current score, that is the Ace's value. If you cannot - it is worth 1.
You can bet on your hand at the beginning of the round to the right when you recieve your initial cards, but you don't have to. We've given you 400 credits to start of with. If you lose on that hand - all of the money you bet will be lost. If you win however, you will double the money you placed down.
We will keep track of how many wins, losses and draws you get below, with the addition of how many risky hits you take (when you take a card above a score of 13), your win rate percentage, the amount of times you unnecessarily stuck at a total in which you could have taken another card and been safe (wrong sticks), the amount of perfect scores you had (21s), and the amount of times you go bust.
To aid your betting endeavors, there is also a handy figure below your cards which details the probability your next card will be safe.
